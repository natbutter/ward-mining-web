BURTORN SILVER

TYR Silver Project

Structurally controlled, silver-rich polymetallic vein system in the New England Orogen, NSW

Project Overview

The TYR Project is located approximately 20 km southwest of Tenterfield, northern New South Wales. The exploration tenement covers 299 km² of highly prospective ground within the New England Orogen — one of Australia's historically productive mineral provinces.

The project hosts over 145 recorded mineral occurrences, including high-grade silver assays from historical workings such as the Ecuador deposit (4,710 ppm Ag) and the Torny Mine (1,420 ppm Ag). Despite this endowment, the project remains largely underexplored for modern, structurally controlled silver-rich polymetallic vein systems.

Primary Resource — Silver

Silver mineralisation at TYR is hosted within Early Permian Bondonga beds, forming two main clusters:

  • Mole River Group — northern tenement cluster, proximal to the Mole Granite contact
  • Clive Group — southern tenement cluster, distal polymetallic Ag-Pb-Zn

Mineralisation is structurally controlled and associated with the emplacement of the Mole Granite, an I-Type Sn-W granite. Classic metal zonation radiates outward from the granite — from proximal tin-tungsten to distal silver, lead, and zinc. Historical mining in the area targeted silver, lead, zinc, arsenic, and tin.

Secondary Resource — Hughs Hill Quartz

The Hughs Hill target represents a large, high-purity silica quartz occurrence within the TYR tenement boundary. Quartz veining is a pervasive feature of the New England Orogen, where significant granite intrusions have driven widespread hydrothermal silicification.

High-purity vein quartz is commercially valuable for:

  • Solar-grade silicon feedstock
  • Semiconductor manufacturing
  • Industrial refractory and abrasive products
  • Optical and specialty glass

Detailed characterisation of purity (Fe, Al, Ti impurities) and resource geometry is being progressed under the current TYR exploration programme.

The TYR Project spans three structural sub-provinces of the New England Orogen:

TELLURIC SANDS

Dilkoon Creek Sand Quarry

Industrial-scale alluvial sand extraction in the Clarence Valley, northern NSW

Project Overview

Telluric Sands is the Group's quarry and industrial minerals division. The flagship project is a proposed sand quarry at Dilkoon Creek, approximately 25 km north of Grafton in the Clarence Valley, NSW. A Development Application (DA) was lodged with the Clarence Valley Council and placed on public exhibition in August 2023.

The project will be determined by the Northern Regional Planning Panel (NRPP), a NSW Government body, and requires approvals from the EPA, DPI Fisheries, and Department of Planning & Environment — Water.

Key Project Parameters

Extraction Rate Up to 200,000 t/yr
Total Resource ~2,000,000 tonnes
Project Life Up to 20 years
Project Area ~37.4 hectares
Operating Hours Mon–Fri 6am–5pm
Sat 6am–3pm
Daily Truck Movements 48 avg / 100 peak

Operations

Sand will be extracted from the alluvial bed of Dilkoon Creek using mechanical excavators. The process involves:

  • In-stream mechanical excavation
  • On-site screening and grading
  • Stockpiling and truck loading for dispatch
  • Progressive rehabilitation of worked areas

The sand deposit formed through flood-transported alluvium from forestry activities in the upper catchment, creating a substantial resource accessible at shallow depths.

Environmental Commitments

An Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) has been prepared covering:

  • Koala management plan (Koalas confirmed on site)
  • Aboriginal Cultural Heritage assessment
  • Noise and traffic impact studies
  • Agricultural land impact assessment
  • Bushfire threat planning
  • Geomorphological assessment of creek bed dynamics
  • Water supply strategy and management plan
  • Restoration and rehabilitation plan

Biodiversity Credits will be paid to offset impacts on 16 identified flora and fauna species.

Geology — Clarence-Moreton Basin

The Dilkoon quarry site sits within the Clarence-Moreton Basin, one of eastern Australia's major Mesozoic sedimentary basins. The basin contains extensive Jurassic and Cretaceous continental sedimentary rocks particularly well-suited to sand and aggregate production.

Key geological formations in the Grafton–Dilkoon corridor:

FormationDescription
Grafton FormationLate Jurassic soft sandstone, siltstone, and claystone
Kangaroo Creek SandstoneWhite/cream saccharoidal sandstone, up to 150 m thick — excellent industrial aggregate
Clarence River AlluviumModern and Quaternary alluvial sand and gravel — the primary resource at Dilkoon Creek

The Dilkoon Creek alluvial deposits represent reworked material derived from Cretaceous and Quaternary fluvial processes, creating clean, well-sorted sand suitable for construction and industrial applications.

TELLURIC EXPLORATIONS #2

Project Generation Vehicle

Identifying and assessing quality mineral opportunities across Australia's mining sector

Our Role

Telluric Explorations #2 serves as the Ward Mining Group's dedicated project generation and mineral opportunity assessment vehicle. Its primary mandate is to systematically locate, evaluate, and acquire high-quality resource projects that align with the Group's strategic commodity and geographic focus.

This entity plays a critical role in ensuring the Group maintains a healthy pipeline of future assets — from grassroots exploration tenements through to near-development projects that can be progressed within the Group's portfolio.

Key Functions

  • Deal Origination — sourcing off-market projects through industry networks, geoscience data review, and government tenement databases
  • Technical Due Diligence — geological assessment of prospectivity, resource potential, and exploration upside
  • Regulatory Review — checking tenement status, native title, and permitting requirements
  • JV & Partnership Structuring — negotiating joint ventures, earn-ins, and acquisition mandates
  • Portfolio Curation — ongoing monitoring of commodity markets and regional opportunity sets

Target Commodities & Regions

Telluric Explorations #2 focuses on commodities aligned with the Group's existing operational expertise and near-term demand growth, including:

Silver Gold Silica / Quartz Industrial Sand Tin & Tungsten Base Metals (Cu, Pb, Zn) Critical Minerals

Geographically, primary focus areas include northern and western NSW — particularly the New England Orogen, Lachlan Fold Belt, and the Clarence-Moreton Basin — with a secondary watch on Queensland's mineral-rich corridor.

Exploration Framework

The company applies a systematic, data-driven methodology to project selection:

01
Regional Target Generation

Desktop review of Geoscience NSW, Geoscience Australia, and publicly available airborne geophysics datasets to identify geological prospectivity across NSW.

02
Field Reconnaissance

Targeted site visits, rock chip sampling, and structural mapping to ground-truth geological interpretations and define drill targets.

03
Tenement Application & Acquisition

Strategic tenement lodgement via the NSW MLAS portal or negotiated acquisitions from third parties aligned with identified targets.

04
Project Incubation or Divestment

Incubate high-value discoveries within the Ward Mining Group portfolio, or seek strategic divestment to realise value at an early stage.
